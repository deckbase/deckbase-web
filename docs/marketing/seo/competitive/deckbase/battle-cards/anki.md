# Battle Card: Anki
*Skill: startup-competitors | Generated: 2026-03-30*

---

## Who They Are
Open-source flashcard system launched in 2006 by Damien Elmes. The gold standard for serious learners — medical students, language learners, and anyone who needs long-term retention. Revenue comes from AnkiMobile iOS ($24.99 one-time); everything else (desktop + AnkiDroid on Android) is free. Maintained by a tiny core team (1–5 people) with a massive open-source community. Natively supports FSRS — the same algorithm Deckbase uses.

**Pricing:** Free (desktop/Android) / $24.99 one-time (iOS AnkiMobile)
**Traction:** apps.ankiweb.net 92K ETV (web only; actual user base 10M+ globally estimated)
**Community:** r/Anki is one of the most active study communities on Reddit

---

## Their Strengths
- Best-in-class FSRS spaced repetition — the same algorithm Deckbase uses, so algorithm quality is not a differentiator here
- Massive shared deck ecosystem — especially AnKing (medical school deck) which is irreplaceable for med students
- Deep customization — 1,000+ add-ons; users build entire study workflows
- Free on desktop and Android — near-zero adoption friction for non-iOS users
- Extreme user loyalty — "Anki identity" is real; power users don't leave
- Trusted brand among serious learners for 20 years

---

## Their Weaknesses
- [Data] "Anki is difficult to use" — top Reddit thread with 40+ comments, Jan 2025
- [Data] "Feels like a 2008 app in 2025" — cited by FlashRecall and other competitors in their marketing
- [Data] iOS $24.99 one-time price causes recurring Reddit complaints: "Why is Anki 24 DOLLARS on App Store"
- No AI card generation — every card is manual. The #1 feature request on Anki forums is "AI from PDF" [Data — Anki Forums, 2025]
- No OCR, no audio blocks natively on mobile, no quiz blocks
- Fragmented mobile experience: iOS (AnkiMobile, paid, different team) vs. Android (AnkiDroid, community-maintained)
- Steep onboarding curve — setup friction filters out casual users
- No AI roadmap — open-source culture is resistant to paid AI feature integration

## When They Win Over You

- **Medical school with AnKing:** The AnKing deck is a community-maintained, medically curated Anki deck that med students pass down between cohorts. There is no equivalent in any other app. Med students on the AnKing workflow will not switch — don't target this segment.
- **Algorithm control freaks:** Anki's FSRS parameters are deeply configurable for experts. Deckbase should support FSRS well, but power users who tune their ease factors manually will prefer Anki.
- **Add-on ecosystem:** 1,000+ community add-ons enable workflows no mobile app can match. Power users who depend on specific add-ons won't give them up.
- **Desktop-first workflows:** Anki's desktop apps (Win/Mac/Linux) are mature and deeply integrated into many users' study setup. If the user primarily studies on desktop, Anki wins.
- **Free forever:** Anki on desktop and Android is free. A price-sensitive user who doesn't need AI may not justify paying for Deckbase.

## Key Vulnerability to Exploit

Every iOS user who Googles "anki alternative iOS" is a potential Deckbase user. The keyword "anki alternative iOS" is essentially uncontested by competitors [Data — DataForSEO]. **Target: iOS users who want Anki's algorithm without the iOS sticker shock or the manual card creation burden.**

## Churn Signals (Why Their Customers Leave)
- iOS $24.99 upfront price — common switching trigger for new iOS users
- "I spend more time making cards than studying" — manual card creation fatigue
- Poor mobile UX — users who study primarily on phone find AnkiMobile frustrating
- Setup complexity — new users who don't invest the time to configure Anki properly give up
- No AI — when users discover AI can generate their cards, staying on manual-only Anki is hard to justify
- "Feels like a 2008 app" — newer entrants actively exploiting this in their marketing

---

## Red Flags
- Anki's loyalty among existing power users (especially medical students) is extremely high. Do not try to convert active AnKing users — this segment has structural barriers no feature set can overcome.

## Yellow Flags
- FSRS is shared with Deckbase, so algorithm quality is a draw — don't lead with "better algorithm than Anki" (because it's not better, it's the same). Lead with "same algorithm, better experience and AI."
