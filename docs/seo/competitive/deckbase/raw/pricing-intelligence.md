**Note [Data]:** There appear to be two pricing tiers — "Plus" (~$35.99/yr, $2.99/mo) and "Plus Unlimited" (~$44.99/yr, $9.99/mo). Pricing varies by account/region. Multiple sources confirm $7.99/mo or $35.99/year as the standard Plus price. [Data — multiple third-party review sites, 2025–2026]

### Pricing Psychology
- **Anchoring:** Plus Unlimited tier makes standard Plus look affordable
- **Decoy:** The free tier's limitations (ads, geo-restricted AI) push users toward paid
- **Charm pricing:** $35.99/year (not $36); $7.99/month
- **Annual lock-in:** 7-day free trial with annual plan; strong lock-in once content is created
- **"Most popular" label:** [Assumption: likely on Plus tier]

### Switching Costs
- **Technical:** High — 700M+ user-generated sets not exportable to competitors in structured format
- **Contractual:** Annual billing; auto-renews
- **Emotional:** Brand ubiquity — "everyone uses Quizlet"

## 2. Anki — Pricing Breakdown

### Pricing Model
- **Value metric:** One-time purchase for iOS; free everywhere else
- **Free tier:** Free on desktop (Windows/Mac/Linux) and Android (AnkiDroid, community app)
- **How it scales:** No scaling — one-time purchase

### Tier Structure
| | Desktop/Android | iOS (AnkiMobile) |
|---|-----------------|------------------|
| Price | Free | $24.99 one-time |
| AI features | Via add-ons | Via add-ons |
| Sync | Free (AnkiWeb) | Free (AnkiWeb) |
| Target persona | Desktop/Android power users | iOS users willing to invest |

**Note [Data]:** AnkiMobile iOS is $24.99 one-time — this is a recurring friction point cited repeatedly on Reddit. "Apple is one of the reasons why there is no free Anki on iOS." [Data — Anki FAQs, Reddit r/Anki]

### Pricing Psychology
- **One-time vs. subscription:** Strong preference signal among power users for no recurring cost
- **No freemium trap:** Once purchased, full access — no AI upsell possible
- **iOS price shock:** $24.99 feels expensive vs. typical app; generates Reddit threads

### Switching Costs
- **Technical:** Extremely high — .apkg deck format, years of review history, FSRS parameters, add-ons
- **Contractual:** None (one-time purchase)
- **Emotional:** "Anki identity" — power users build their entire study life around Anki

## Pricing Landscape Summary

- **Dominant value metric:** Flat freemium subscription (free tier + monthly/annual premium)
- **Price range:** $0 (free tiers) to ~$7.99/mo for premium; $35.99–$72/yr for annual
- **Median price point:** ~$5/mo or $60/yr for comparable premium tiers
- **Free tier prevalence:** 100% of direct competitors offer free tiers — free is table stakes in this market
- **Annual discount range:** 50–70% off monthly pricing (strong annual lock-in incentive)

### Pricing Whitespace for Deckbase
1. **Lower annual price:** Quizlet charges $35.99/yr; positioning Deckbase at $29.99/yr or $24.99/yr could undercut while maintaining perceived quality
2. **iOS users escaping Anki's $24.99:** iOS-first users who don't want to pay $24.99 upfront but want FSRS are underserved — a $4.99/mo subscription with free trial hits this gap
3. **No AI add-on upsell:** Competitors either include AI in premium or limit it heavily. Bundling AI generation + FSRS in one clean freemium tier is differentiated.

### Switching Cost Patterns
- **High:** Anki (years of review history, add-ons), RemNote (linked notes), Quizlet (social/shared sets)
- **Low:** Knowt, Mochi, Brainscape (less lock-in)
- **Deckbase's edge:** Anki import (.apkg) dramatically reduces switching cost from the largest competitor with high technical switching costs
